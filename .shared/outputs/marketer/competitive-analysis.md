## 1. Duolingo (Language Learning)

**What they do:** Gamified language learning app with bite-sized lessons, streaks, hearts, leaderboards, and spaced repetition. 800M+ downloads. $8B+ market cap. Proven that gamified practice loops drive engagement and retention at massive scale.

**What they lack:**
- No body language or communication skills category
- No webcam/video-based practice
- No AI visual feedback (their AI is text/speech-based)
- No enterprise/B2B offering for professional development
- Content is language-specific, not transferable to soft skills

**How seeneyu is different:**
- seeneyu applies the exact Duolingo model (practice loop + gamification + streaks + hearts + leaderboards + leagues) to body language — a category Duolingo does not serve
- seeneyu adds webcam recording + AI visual analysis, which Duolingo's text/audio model cannot support
- seeneyu's gamification engine (XP 25-100/activity, 5-tier leagues, 30+ badges, daily quests, combo multipliers) is explicitly modeled on Duolingo's proven engagement mechanics
- The B2B licensing opportunity (Learning Assistant Engine) gives seeneyu a revenue path Duolingo does not pursue

**Verdict:** Duolingo validates the model. seeneyu applies it to an unserved category with defensible AI technology.

## 2. Coursera / Udemy (Video Courses)

**What they do:** Marketplace platforms with thousands of video courses, including soft skills and communication categories. Coursera partners with universities; Udemy is instructor-led. Both offer certificates. Udemy Business and Coursera for Business serve enterprise L&D.

**What they lack:**
- Entirely passive — learners watch videos but never practice
- No recording, no feedback, no measurement of improvement
- No AI analysis of learner performance
- Completion rates are notoriously low (5-15% for MOOCs)
- Soft skills courses are generic (e.g., "Body Language for Leaders" is 2 hours of theory)
- No gamification beyond basic progress bars and certificates

**How seeneyu is different:**
- seeneyu's core loop is active practice, not passive watching — every clip requires recording and gets AI feedback
- Micro-practice (30s steps) keeps engagement high vs. 2-hour lecture fatigue
- MediaPipe scoring gives objective, measurable improvement metrics (not just "course completed")
- Gamification (streaks, hearts, quests, leaderboards) drives daily return visits vs. one-time course purchase
- Foundation courses provide theory, but always paired with hands-on practice clips

**Verdict:** Coursera/Udemy own passive content. seeneyu owns active practice. They could be complementary — a Coursera course could recommend seeneyu for the practice component.

## 3. Orai (Speech Coaching)

**What they do:** Mobile AI speech coach. Users record themselves speaking and receive feedback on pace, filler words, energy, and clarity. $2.3M seed raised (2021). Primarily targets public speakers and presenters.

**What they lack:**
- Speech metrics only — no body language, facial expressions, posture, or gesture analysis
- Mobile-only — no desktop web experience, no webcam-based practice
- No AI Vision — cannot analyze visual signals from video
- No exemplar-based learning — users practice in a vacuum, not from curated performances
- No gamification system (no XP, streaks, leaderboards)
- No enterprise/B2B product
- Limited content — users must self-direct what to practice

**How seeneyu is different:**
- seeneyu analyzes body language via webcam (MediaPipe FaceLandmarker + PoseLandmarker), not just voice
- Curated Hollywood clips serve as exemplars — users know exactly what "good" looks like before practicing
- Full gamification engine drives retention (Orai has no engagement loop beyond raw practice)
- Web + mobile (React Native) vs. mobile-only
- Coach Ney AI assistant provides context-aware coaching, not just metric readouts
- 5 mini-games create viral acquisition channels that Orai lacks

**Verdict:** Orai proved there is demand for AI coaching. seeneyu is a generation ahead with visual AI, exemplar-based learning, and gamification.

## 4. Poised (Communication Coaching)

**What they do:** Real-time communication coaching overlay for Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams. Provides live feedback on filler words, pace, energy, confidence, and empathy during actual calls. $10.7M Series A (2023).

**What they lack:**
- Live-call feedback only — no dedicated practice mode
- Speech metrics only — no body language, posture, or facial expression analysis despite being on video calls
- No exemplar-based learning — never shows what "good" looks like
- No recording + review workflow — feedback is ephemeral during the call
- No gamification, no skill progression tracking
- Narrow use case: only works during meetings, useless for preparation
- Cannot be used for interview prep, presentation rehearsal, or deliberate practice

**How seeneyu is different:**
- seeneyu is a training platform — practice before the meeting, not during it
- Analyzes full body language (face, posture, gesture, gaze), not just speech patterns
- Exemplar clips teach by demonstration — see it, then replicate it
- Persistent progress tracking with XP, levels, and skill scores vs. ephemeral per-call feedback
- Works for interview prep, presentation rehearsal, dating confidence, sales training — not just meetings
- Coach Ney provides coaching conversations, not just live metric overlays

**Verdict:** Poised is the "during the game" coach. seeneyu is the "training gym." Both have value, but seeneyu addresses the larger opportunity (skill development) vs. the narrower one (in-call optimization).

---

## 5. BetterUp (Executive Coaching)

**What they do:** Premium executive coaching platform connecting professionals with certified human coaches. 1:1 sessions via video call. Corporate clients include Google, Snap, Hilton. $4.7B peak valuation. Raised $300M+ total.

**What they lack:**
- $300+/month minimum — inaccessible to 99% of professionals
- Entirely human-dependent — cannot scale without hiring more coaches
- No technology moat — human coaches use generic video calls
- No self-serve practice — coaching happens only during scheduled sessions
- No AI feedback between sessions
- No body language-specific analysis — coaches provide subjective observations
- No gamification or daily engagement mechanics

**How seeneyu is different:**
- seeneyu starts at $0 (Basic) and caps at $24/month (Advanced) vs. $300+/month
- AI-powered, infinitely scalable — no human bottleneck
- 24/7 availability — practice anytime, not on a coach's schedule
- Objective, measurable feedback via MediaPipe (scores, timestamps, specific posture metrics) vs. subjective observations
- Gamification drives daily practice; BetterUp's model is weekly/biweekly sessions
- seeneyu could serve as a complement — daily practice tool between BetterUp coaching sessions

**Verdict:** BetterUp owns the premium enterprise coaching market. seeneyu democratizes it. Different price points, different delivery models. seeneyu is the "gym membership" to BetterUp's "personal trainer."

## Defensibility Moats

1. **Data moat:** Every practice recording, mini-game submission, and expression score contributes to a proprietary body language dataset. No competitor is collecting this data.
2. **Content moat:** 65+ curated clips with observation guides, practice steps, and screenplay context. Each clip is a multi-hour curation effort.
3. **Engine moat:** Learning Assistant Engine is abstracted and SaaS-ready. Can be licensed to other edtech platforms, creating a platform lock-in.
4. **Category moat:** First mover in "AI body language practice." The term barely exists. seeneyu defines the category.
5. **Cost moat:** MediaPipe runs client-side at zero per-user API cost. Competitors using server-side AI analysis pay per request.
