## Persona 1: Maya Chen — Young Professional Preparing for Job Interviews

### Demographics
- **Age:** 27
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA
- **Role:** UX Designer at a mid-stage startup, looking to move to a senior role at a larger company
- **Income:** $95,000/year
- **Education:** B.A. Design, M.S. HCI

### Goals
- Land a senior UX design role at a top-tier tech company (Google, Apple, Figma)
- Project confidence during behavioral interviews, design critiques, and whiteboard sessions
- Eliminate nervous habits: fidgeting, breaking eye contact, speaking too fast under pressure
- Build a portfolio presentation she can deliver with executive-level composure

### Pain Points
- Has strong technical skills but consistently receives feedback that she "doesn't seem confident enough" in interviews
- Watched YouTube videos on body language but never practiced — knows the theory, cannot execute
- Tried Toastmasters but the weekly scheduling conflicted with her work hours
- Cannot afford a $300/month executive coach on her current salary
- Feels awkward practicing in front of a mirror with no feedback on what specifically to improve

### How seeneyu Helps
- **Foundation courses** teach her the theory behind eye contact, posture, and vocal pacing in structured 10-minute lessons
- **Clip library** shows her exactly how confident speakers hold themselves — not abstract advice but specific, observable behaviors
- **Micro-practice** lets her practice one skill at a time in 30-second chunks (eye contact for 30s, then posture for 30s) with instant AI feedback
- **MediaPipe scoring** tells her precisely: "Your gaze dropped at 0:04 — try holding for 2.5 seconds" instead of vague "improve eye contact"
- **Coach Ney** provides on-demand voice coaching: "Maya, your posture opened up 40% since last week. For your next interview, focus on maintaining that open stance when you're asked about failures"
- **Streaks and XP** keep her practicing daily — 15 minutes/day for 2 weeks before her interview
- **Submission review** lets her watch her progress over time: Day 1 vs. Day 14 side-by-side

### Willingness to Pay
- Would pay $12/month (Standard tier) without hesitation — it is less than one career coaching session and more useful than any YouTube video
- Would upgrade to $24/month (Advanced) in the 2 weeks before a major interview for longer recordings and monthly coach summary
- Price sensitivity: moderate. Would not pay $50+/month but sees $12-24 as an easy investment in career advancement

### Acquisition Channel

- Signs up for free tier, converts to Standard after 3 days

### Demographics
- **Age:** 23
- **Location:** Madrid, Spain (studying remotely for a US master's program)
- **Role:** Graduate student in Business Analytics, seeking US internship

### Goals
- Communicate confidently in English-language job interviews with US companies
- Overcome the "double barrier" of language + cultural body language norms (Spanish vs. American professional norms differ significantly in eye contact duration, personal space, and gesture expressiveness)

### Pain Points
- Language apps (Duolingo, Babbel) teach vocabulary and grammar but zero about the non-verbal communication that Americans expect in professional settings
- American eye contact norms (longer, more direct) feel uncomfortable — but he knows breaking eye contact signals low confidence to US interviewers
- Video interview platforms (HireVue) grade him partly on non-verbal cues, but he has no way to practice those cues
- Cannot afford private coaching on a student budget
- Watches American movies and notices differences in body language but has no structured way to learn and practice those norms

### How seeneyu Helps
- **Hollywood clips curated for specific skills** expose him to American professional body language norms demonstrated by skilled performers — not actors "acting," but characters executing the exact non-verbal skills employers evaluate
- **Foundation courses** provide structured learning: "Eye Contact in Professional Settings" teaches him that Americans expect 2-3 seconds of direct gaze vs. the shorter glances common in Spanish culture
- **Micro-practice with AI feedback** lets him build new body language habits gradually: 30-second exercises that slowly extend his comfortable eye contact duration from 1s to 2.5s over 2 weeks
- **Gamification** makes daily practice feel like a game, not homework — streaks, XP, and leaderboards keep him engaged alongside his demanding coursework
- **Mini-games** (Guess Expression, Match Expression) build his ability to read American facial expressions, which differ subtly from Spanish norms in professional contexts
- **Free tier** gives him access on a student budget; Standard ($12/month) is feasible for the 2 months before internship interview season

## Implications for Product Prioritization

1. **Interview prep mode** (Maya, Tomas): Most common entry point. Consider a dedicated "/interview-prep" landing page with curated clips for the 5 most-asked behavioral questions.

2. **B2B team features** (David, Rachel): Highest revenue potential. Team dashboards, custom scenario upload, and SSO should be prioritized for Phase 3 GTM.

3. **Cross-cultural body language content** (Tomas): Underserved market. ESL students are a massive global segment. Consider adding clips that explicitly contrast body language norms across cultures.

4. **Pre-event warmup mode** (Priya): Quick 5-minute warmup routine before presentations. Could be a standalone feature: "Stage Ready in 5 Minutes."

5. **Sales-specific tactics** (Rachel): Sales teams are high-LTV buyers. Consider partnering with sales enablement platforms (Gong, Chorus, Salesloft) to embed seeneyu practice into sales workflows.
